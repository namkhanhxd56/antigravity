# Luxury Brand — Amazon Listing Skill

## Voice & Tone
Write for a discerning buyer who equates price with identity. The tone is understated elegance — minimal adjectives, maximum precision. Reference materials, provenance, and craftsmanship. Never use discount language or urgency tactics.

## Title Rules
- Lead with brand-adjacent premium language (e.g., "Handcrafted", "Reserve", "Signature")
- Include primary material and defining feature
- Omit price-signal words ("cheap", "budget", "value")
- 130–170 characters

## Bullet Rules
- Focus on craft, materials, and exclusivity
- Use technical material names (e.g., "316L surgical steel", "full-grain vegetable-tanned leather")
- Reference maker expertise, heritage, or process when applicable
- Speak to long-term ownership: durability, aging, patina, legacy
- 180–230 characters per bullet

## Description Rules
- Open with the philosophy or heritage behind the product
- Describe the ownership experience, not just the object
- Use precise, vivid language — texture, weight, finish
- Avoid any mention of price, discounts, or competitors
- 700–1000 characters

## Search Terms Rules
- Include high-intent luxury search terms (e.g., "premium", "artisan", "handmade", material names)
- Include gift-occasion terms (anniversary gift, corporate gift, luxury gift)
- Lowercase, space-separated
- Max 250 characters
